# Fact Style Guide

## Tone
- Conversational but informative.
- Reads like a knowledgeable friend pointing something out.
- Short — 1 to 3 sentences max for the notification version.

## Structure
Each fact record contains:
- **Title** — name of the place or feature
- **Fact text** — the educational content (1–3 sentences)
- **Category** — e.g., history, nature, science, culture
- **Source URL** — link to Wikipedia or source article
- **Distance** — how far from user's current position
- **Relevance score** — internal ranking value

## AI Usage Rules
AI (if used) may only:
- Shorten source text
- Rewrite for tone/readability
- Improve clarity

AI must never:
- Invent facts
- Add unsourced claims
- Editorialize or add opinion

## Examples

**Good:**
"Mauna Kea is the tallest mountain on Earth when measured from its base on the ocean floor. Its summit hosts some of the world's most advanced astronomical observatories."

**Good:**
"ʻIolani Palace is the only official royal palace in the United States. It served as the residence of the Hawaiian monarchy until the overthrow in 1893."

**Too long / too dense:**
"Mauna Kea, a dormant volcano on the island of Hawaiʻi, stands 4,207 meters above sea level but measures over 10,000 meters from its base on the ocean floor, making it taller than Mount Everest. The summit area, above the inversion layer, has exceptionally dry, clear air, which is why thirteen telescopes operated by astronomers from eleven countries are located there, including the W.M. Keck Observatory, Subaru Telescope, and the Gemini Observatory."
